Sébastien Destercke


Candidate for: At-large member


BIO

Sébastien Destercke is a Full CNRS researcher at UTC, France. He graduated in 2004 as an engineer from the Faculté Polytechnique de Mons in Belgium. In 2008, he earned a Ph.D. degree in computer science from Université Paul Sabatier, in Toulouse (France). His main research interests are in the field of uncertainty reasoning with imprecision-tolerant models (DS theory, imprecise probabilities, possibility theory, ...), with a focus on issues related to reliability and risk analysis, decision making and machine learning. His past actions to support IP activities include the organization of conferences and special sessions focusing on imprecision, among others.


STATEMENT

I think that imprecise probabilistic approaches, or more generally approaches trying to model limited information, can shed interesting lights in many problems. I also think that recent developments have readied IP to tackle really complex applications or problems issued from other scientific communities, and that we need to reinforce links with such communities. At the same time, I also have the feeling that we should try to better coordinate and connect the somewhat scattered efforts to promote the modelling of imprecision in uncertainty. As a member of ISIPTA EC, I intend to actively support actions going in such directions such as, e.g., the organisation of a dedicated Daghstul seminar or networking proposals.
