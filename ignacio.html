Ignacio Montes


Candidate for: Executive editor, At-large member


BIO

Ignacio Montes received the BsC on Mathematics from the University of Oviedo in 2009, where he also got his PhD degree on Mathematics and Statistics in May 2014. Currently, he is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Statistics and Operations Research at the University of Oviedo. During his PhD, he started working in the field of Imprecise Probabilities, which is currently his main topic of research. He has participated in the SIPTA Summer School in 2010 and 2012, and the ISIPTA conferences from 2013 on. Also, he has organised a special session at IPMU’2018 related to IP, and he has been a member of the organising committee of the SIPTA Summer School 2018 and WPMSIIP 2018. More information is available on my website.


STATEMENT

Since I started working in the field of Imprecise Probabilities, I have regularly participated in the SIPTA events. In order to continue with the advances achieved by the previous Executive Committees of SIPTA, I think that we should increase our visibility in two main directions: on the one hand, participating regularly in conferences/workshops related to close topics. In this way, we can easily disseminate our result to researchers whose interests are not far from ours. On the other hand, doing an effort to increase the visibility of the results obtained by the members of the community. Undoubtedly, IP-researchers are doing significant advances both from the theoretical and applied viewpoints, but I still think that we should do an extra effort to bring these results closer to other researchers unfamiliar to IP.
