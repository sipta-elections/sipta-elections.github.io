Jasper De Bock


Candidate for: President, At-large member


BIO

I am an assistant professor at Ghent University, Belgium, and have conducted research on imprecise probabilities (IP) for about eight years now. I was an at-large member of SIPTA’s Executive Committee for the past four years, and I am the main organizer of the forthcoming anniversary edition of ISIPTA, to take place this summer in Ghent. More biographic info is available on my website.


STATEMENT

I am running for president of SIPTA for the same reason I decided to organize ISIPTA 2019: I am passionate about IP and its community, and I am willing to spend time and effort to facilitate its development. If elected, I intend to focus on attracting new members to our community. One method to achieve this, which turned out to work very well for ISIPTA 2019, is advertisement. This includes traditional methods such as composing large mailing lists and preparing all sorts of advertising material, but what I believe has had the largest impact, was very personal advertisement. In particular, I identified potentially interested researchers, read their work, and sent out individual emails to each of them explaining why I think their work belongs at ISIPTA. This effort was well worth it: ISIPTA 2019 had more than 60 paper submissions, thereby completely turning around the downwards trend. Setting up such an advertisement campaign was very time-consuming though, and I don’t think it should be the responsibility of the ISIPTA organizers alone. If elected, I therefore intend for the SIPTA society to develop a sustainable advertising strategy that future ISIPTA editions (and other SIPTA events) can benefit from. This also includes strengthening SIPTA’s (online) identity and establishing it as a brand with international reputation.

Another method for attracting new members that I think should be explored, is to organize workshops on various mainstream topics where IP could have a substantial impact. Consider for example a workshop on robust AI with participants from our community as well as that of (robust) AI, with the aim of exchanging ideas and exploring options for joint research. Not only would such events be of independent interest, they would also allow us to let researchers from other fields experience what IP and SIPTA have to offer, and to attract them and their colleagues to our community. I think it would be great if we could establish a series of such thematic workshops; let’s call them SIPTA workshops, to strengthen the SIPTA brand even further. A crucial first step towards achieving this will be to find ways of (partially) funding such workshops. COST actions are a great tool to do so, and Dagstuhl seminars would be too, but we should also actively seek out alternatives.

If you like these ideas for attracting new members to our community, and if you are willing to give me the chance to develop them, then I would be honoured to serve as president of the SIPTA society. If not, or if a more senior president is deemed preferable (I am indeed quite young to be the face of an organization), then I would be happy to continue to serve the SIPTA society as an at-large member of its executive committee.
